(1.4) Financial Statements

There are four types of financial statements that will be focussed on in this chapter

  • Statement of Income:Presents revenues and expenses of a company for a specific period of time.
  • Statement of Changes in Equity:Summarizes the changes in shareholders equity that have occured for a specific period of time especially those that are related to the issue of shares, generation of net income, and declaration of dividends.
  • Statement of Financial Position:Reports the Assets, liabilities, and the shareholders' equity of a business at a specific date.
  • Statement of Cash Flows:Summarizes information about the cash inflows and outflows for a specific period of time.

(2.2) Identifying Ratios Relative to the companies liquidity, solvency and profitability

We can analyse a companies liquidity, solvency and profitability using ratio analysis

Data Analytics: The Process of analysing enourmous amounts of data to find patterns and correlations, trends and other valuable insights to enhance descision-making. This includes descisions that will impact financial reporting.

  • Using the statement of financial position
  • Using Statement of income

3 types of Ratios

  • Intracompany Comparisons: Covering two or more periods for the same company.
  • Intercompany Comparisons: Based on comparisons with a competitor in the same industry.
  • Industry Comparisons: Based on comparisons to average ratios for the industry that a company operates in.

Liquidity

A companies ability to pay its obligations that are due for payment within the next year. You would use liquidity ratios to look closely at the relationship of its current assets to its current liabilities.

Working Capital: Difference between Current Assets and Current Liabilities

Working Capital = Current Assets - Current Liabilities

Current Ratio: a ratio between the current assets to liabilities

Current Ratio = Current Assets / Current Liabilities

Solvency

Measure a company’s ability to survive over the long term by having enough assets to settle its liabilities as they fall due.

Debts to Total Assets: measure a company’s ability to survive over the long term by having enough assets to settle its liabilities as they fall due.

Debt to Total Assets = Total Liabilities / Total Assets

Profitability

Measure a company’s operating success over time in specific periods, usually one year.

Basic earnings Per Share (EPS): Measures the income earned on each common share

Basic earnings Per Share = Income Available to Common Shareholders / Weighted Average Number of Common Shares

Price earnings Ratio: The price-earnings ratio is a frequently quoted statistic that is the ratio of the common share price in the stock market to its basic earnings per share.

Price Earnings Ratio = Market Price Per Share / Basic earnings Per Share
